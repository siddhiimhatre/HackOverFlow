Milky-Way Facts

Milky-Way Profile

Parameter Details
Type: Barred Spiral
Distance to Galactic Centre : 27,000 ly
Diameter : 100,000 - 180,000 ly
Mass: 800 - 1,500 M☉
Age: 13.6 Billion years
Number of Stars: 100 - 400 billion
Constellation: Sagittarius

Facts About the Milky Way:

1. The Milky Way began as a series of dense regions in the early universe not long after the Big Bang.

2. The Milky Way moves through space at a velocity of about 552 kilometres per second (343 miles per second)
with respect to the Cosmic Microwave Background radiation.

3.The Milky Way’s central core contains a supermassive black hole. It is commonly referred to as Sagittarius A*

4. Our galaxy will collide with Andromeda Galaxy in about 5 billion years. Some astronomers refer to our
two galaxy as a binary system of giant spirals.

5. The width of the path of totality is usually about 160 km across and can sweep across an area of Earth’s
surface about 10,000 miles long.





Univesre From Nothing

Space is usually regarded as being completely empty. But this is not true. The vast gaps between the stars and planets are filled with huge amounts of thinly spread gas and dust. Even the emptiest parts of space contain at least a few hundred atoms or molecules per cubic metre.
